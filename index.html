Horses and Climate Change – What Does the Future Hold? Final Part 4 – by Ashley Lorinsky

Now let’s finish up and talk about cold weather. Now yes our winters are becoming warmer and the temperatures have not been as cold as what they have been in the past.

But it does not mean an end to winter in some areas just yet. Some areas, yes, may no longer get snow or get that cold but we are not quite there yet.

Extreme precipitation events – like major winter snowstorms and lake-effect snow events – are actually becoming more common because of that increased evaporation of moisture into the atmosphere and an unstable jet stream. The warming from climate change may be causing the jet stream to become unstable resulting in its winds veering off from its usual path around the Arctic.

Read Post

More research is still being done to understand this phenomenon but when this happens frigidly cold air, or your polar vortexes occur, spilling into areas not accustomed to seeing such low temperatures for such long periods. 

Major snowstorms and frigid temperatures may sound scary but our horses do do better in cold weather, again, only up to a point!

When we get extreme cold snaps horses will seek protection from the wind, huddle together to stay warm, eat more, not move much, and sometimes drink less. Since they may not move as much, they will have excess energy and that is where they will seem frisky to us.

Horses that have metabolic diseases or insulin resistance have a compromised ability to regulate their body systems.

And horses that have recently been sick won’t be able to handle cold the way they normally could. So we have to pay close attention to these horses during these extreme cold snaps to make sure they are okay.

Blanketing, warm mashes, good shelters to protect from the wind, and access to forage will go a long way in helping out horses through intense cold periods. Just as with humans, it is a lot easier for horses to get and stay warm than it is to get and stay cool!

The horse industry and us individually as horse owners have a lot that we should and need to do to help our horses with climate change. But the things we can do are doable!

We just need to get up and start for the sake of our horses!
